Sudoku


Game Overview:

The Sudoku game consists of a 9x9 grid where players input numbers from 1 to 9 in such a way that each row, column, and 3x3 subgrid contains all the numbers from 1 to 9 without repetition. The game provides buttons to start and stop a timer, a button to check the current state of the grid, and displays the number of mistakes made and the progress of the player.

Game Parameters:

Timer: The game includes a timer that tracks the elapsed time from when the player starts the game until they finish or stop the timer.

Mistakes: The game keeps track of the number of mistakes the player makes while entering numbers into the grid. Mistakes are counted when the player enters a number that violates the Sudoku rules, such as repeating a number in the same row, column, or 3x3 subgrid.

Progress: The game displays the player's progress as a percentage, indicating how much of the puzzle they have completed relative to the total number of empty cells in the grid. This progress metric gives players feedback on their advancement toward completing the Sudoku puzzle.

Cognitive Skills Enhancement:

Logic: Players need to apply logical deduction and reasoning skills to solve the Sudoku puzzle by filling in the grid with numbers while adhering to the game's rules.

Problem-Solving: Players must employ problem-solving strategies to identify and correct mistakes in their solutions, ensuring that each row, column, and subgrid contains all the required numbers.

Memory: Players may benefit from memory recall as they remember previously entered numbers and use them to make decisions about where to place subsequent numbers in the grid.

Brainwave Frequencies Targeted:

Beta (12-30 Hz): During gameplay, players may experience increased beta wave activity as they engage in focused attention, problem-solving, and decision-making tasks.

Gamma (30-100 Hz): Gamma wave activity may also be stimulated during moments of heightened concentration and insight when players successfully solve portions of the puzzle or recognize patterns within the grid.
